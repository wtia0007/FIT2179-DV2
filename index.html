I. Geographic and Sectoral Context

The map below illustrates the intensity of tree cover loss across Malaysia. Sarawak and Sabah consistently show higher loss levels, largely driven by large-scale land conversion. By observing the Donut and Area charts, we can see that Permanent Agriculture and Logging remain the dominant forces behind this change.

II. Economic Impetus: The Palm Oil Factor

As a global leader in palm oil production, Malaysia's economy is tightly linked to commodity prices. The Scatter Plot shows a positive correlation between price spikes and production increases. The Bubble Chart highlights how "hot years" (high price) often align with periods of significant land use pressure.

III. Regional Vulnerability & Protection

Different states employ different land management strategies. While the Lollipop Chart shows the remaining forest reserves, the Stacked Proportion chart reveals that in states like Pahang, the relative impact of "Logging" is much higher than in others.
